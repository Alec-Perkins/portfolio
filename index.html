Spring-Mass System Simulation with MATLAB - Nov. 2023


Given a system of two masses and three springs connected in alternating series. The masses are connected by the springs to walls on either side of the apparatus. The system is initially at rest in equilibrium and then set in motion by displacing the masses arbitrarily. In order to simulate this type of movement, eigenvalue and eigenvector analysis was used to create equations for motion which was later plotted in MATLAB.

Microscope / Telescope Eyepiece Mount - Jun. 2023


This project was created to assist in taking high-quality photos through a microscope or telescope eyepiece with a cellphone. The mount fastens to the eyepiece and cellphone while the x-axis and y-axis positions can be adjusted with a wireless remote to adjust either of the two stepper motor linear rail actuators in order to get the phone lens lined up perfectly with the eyepiece.

Compressed Air Motor (CNC) - May 2023


This project was created in my machine shop class ME12s, where we were taught how to construct parts for a compressed air motor, first in solidworks, and then in CNC mills and conventional lathes to create each piece of our motor with the required tolerances. My motor was able to reach 3k RPM after being fully constructed and polished, which exceeded the required RMP in order to get full credit for this project in the class. I enjoyed learning how to operate the various CNC machines and manual tools and plan to continue using these tools for my own future personal projects.


Observed Microorganism - Jan. 2023


Sampling some stagnant water from the UCSB lagoon, I was able to observe a microorgansm collecting nutrients from the algea that grows in the lagoon water. This imagery and video was at around 100x magnification. I was later able to use my documentation to identify this organsim as a Bdelloid rotifer, Rotaria rotatoria.

Tensegrity Chair - Jun. 2022


I constructed, sanded, and tested a special kind of chair (Tensegrity Chair) that appears to be floating without support but uses illusion and static physics’s forces as well as internal force calculations for the load to be fully supported and balanced by each force of compression and tension in the cables and a steel turnbuckle joining the top and bottom half of the chair together. I then tested the load of the turnbuckle by incrementing the weight applied to the chair until the turnbuckle failed. These tests are documented and tabulated in the full report.
